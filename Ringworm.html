Ringworm

A common fungal infection that affects the skin, creating ring-shaped, red, and scaly patches. Despite its name, it's not caused by worms but by fungi and is highly contagious

Symptoms

  • Ring-shaped, red, scaly patches
  • Clear or healthy skin in the center
  • Itchy, burning sensation
  • Raised, bumpy border around patches
  • Hair loss in affected areas
  • Patches may overlap and spread

It can appears in:

  • Scalp and beard area
  • Arms and legs
  • Torso and back
  • Groin area
  • Feet (athlete's foot)
  • Between fingers and toes
